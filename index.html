About The Swan

Nestled in the heart of Beaconsfield, The Swan has been a cornerstone of the local community for over 175 years. Our traditional British pub combines the charm of yesteryear with modern comfort, offering an authentic experience that celebrates the finest of British pub culture.

We pride ourselves on serving locally sourced ingredients, traditional ales from regional breweries, and maintaining the warm, welcoming atmosphere that has made us a beloved gathering place for generations of locals and visitors alike.

Whether you're joining us for a quiet pint after work, a hearty Sunday roast with the family, or celebrating a special occasion, The Swan offers an experience steeped in tradition and delivered with genuine hospitality.

Our History

Est. 1847

The Swan first opened its doors in 1847 as a coaching inn, serving weary travelers on the road between London and the countryside. The original building, constructed of local stone and timber, featured stables for horses and lodging for guests.

Victorian Era

During the Victorian period, The Swan became the social heart of Beaconsfield. The pub hosted local meetings, celebrations, and served as an unofficial town hall where important community decisions were made over pints of ale.

20th Century

Through two world wars, The Swan remained a constant in the community. During WWII, it served as a gathering place for locals and stationed servicemen, maintaining morale during the darkest of times.

Modern Day

Today, The Swan continues its proud tradition while embracing modern hospitality standards. We've carefully preserved the original character while updating facilities to ensure comfort for all our guests.
